About

Taste is a technical constraint.

Good personal software needs engineering judgment, but it also needs a point of view: what to show, what to hide, what to make slower, what to make obvious.

I like building things that feel inhabited: clear enough to use, specific enough to belong to someone, and weird enough to keep thinking about.
01
Start with behavior.

A page can look beautiful in a screenshot and still be inert. The better question is what it does when someone returns.

02
Make the invisible legible.

State, rhythm, constraints, and affordances should be felt through the interface before they need to be explained.

03
Keep the edge human.

Even technical work benefits from texture: good names, calm defaults, forgiving controls, and a little wit.
